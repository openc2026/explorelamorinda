Sleepy Hollow is Orinda's crown jewel. Tucked into a secluded valley backed by Briones Regional Park, it combines privacy, prestige, and a tight-knit community centered on its legendary elementary school and swim & tennis club. Homes range from $2M to $12M+ — and they move fast.

## The Character

Sleepy Hollow feels like a secret, even though it's five minutes from downtown Orinda. The single-entry valley creates natural seclusion; the backdrop of Briones wilderness adds drama. It's genuinely beautiful in a way that's rare this close to BART and San Francisco.

But Sleepy Hollow isn't just about the setting — it's about community. The elementary school is the best in Orinda (which is saying something). The Sleepy Hollow Swim & Tennis Club anchors summer life. Parents know each other; kids grow up together. It's the kind of neighborhood people move mountains to get into.

## Typical Homes

- **Style:** Ranch, Craftsman, Colonial, Mediterranean, Modern — wide variety
- **Size:** 2,500–6,000+ sq ft
- **Lots:** Large (0.5–2+ acres common)
- **Condition:** Ranges from original 1960s homes to spectacular new construction

The housing stock is diverse. You'll find charming original ranches alongside $10M+ custom estates. What unites them is the location and the lot sizes — Sleepy Hollow has space.

## Price Range

**$2M – $12M+**

The median sale price hovers around $3M. Entry-level homes (smaller, older, or needing work) can start around $2M. Premium properties — hilltop locations, large lots, new construction — range from $5M to $12M and beyond.

Sleepy Hollow homes typically sell above list price (often 10%+) and go pending within a week.

## Who Lives Here

- **Affluent families with children** — the dominant demographic
- **Tech executives and entrepreneurs** — especially from SF/Oakland
- **Partners at major law and consulting firms**
- **Some empty nesters** who raised kids here and can't leave

Sleepy Hollow families tend to be high-achieving, education-focused, and willing to pay for quality of life.

## Walkability & Amenities

**Walkability: Moderate**

- Walk to Sleepy Hollow Elementary (the neighborhood school)
- Walk to Sleepy Hollow Swim & Tennis Club
- Drive to downtown Orinda, BART, and everything else
- Excellent hiking access to Briones Regional Park

Within the valley, kids can bike to school and the pool. Beyond the valley, you'll drive.

## The School

**Sleepy Hollow Elementary** is routinely rated among the best in California. It's the primary reason many families target this neighborhood — you can't attend without living in the catchment area.

## The Club

**Sleepy Hollow Swim & Tennis Club** (separate from home purchase):
- Large pool complex
- Tennis courts
- Summer swim team (highly competitive, highly social)
- Community events year-round

The club is central to Sleepy Hollow life, especially in summer. Membership waitlists can be long.

## The Vibe

Sleepy Hollow is intense about the things it values: kids, schools, community, achievement. It's friendly but competitive — swim team times matter, school fundraiser participation matters, and yes, your address matters. 

That intensity isn't for everyone. But for families who want the best schools, a safe place for kids to roam, and neighbors who share their values, Sleepy Hollow delivers.

## Best Time to Visit

**Late March through early May** showcases Sleepy Hollow at its most beautiful. The hills backing the valley turn deep green, wildflowers bloom on the Briones trails, and the secluded setting feels almost magical with California's spring light filtering through the oaks.

Drive through on a weekday afternoon around 3pm to see school pickup at Sleepy Hollow Elementary — that's when the neighborhood's family-centered character is most visible. The pool and tennis club come alive as weather warms, but the real energy builds through April and May as swim team practice starts.

Spring is also when Sleepy Hollow homes most frequently come to market. Families list after accepting their kids into next year's schools, making March-May the window for serious buyers.

## Considerations

- **Premium pricing** — entry point is $2M+
- **Competitive culture** — high-achieving families can mean pressure
- **Single access road** — traffic at school drop-off/pick-up
- **Fire risk** — interface with wildland (mitigated but real)
