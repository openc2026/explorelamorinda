## Site improvement

**`content/getting-around/caldecott-tunnel.md`** — new dedicated page (13.2 KB)

- The `getting-around/` directory previously had only `_index.md`. The Caldecott Tunnel is the single biggest navigational variable in Lamorinda life and deserved a standalone page.
- Real research, verified against Wikipedia and 511 CCTA:
  - Four bores, opening dates: Bores 1 & 2 = 1937, Bore 3 = 1964, Bore 4 = 2013
  - Thomas E. Caldecott (1878–1951): mayor of Berkeley 1930–1932, Alameda County Supervisor 1933–1945, president of Joint Highway District 13
  - Current configuration: Bores 1 & 2 carry eastbound, Bores 3 & 4 carry westbound (the reversible middle bore was eliminated when Bore 4 opened in 2013)
  - Daily volume: 170,000–180,000 vehicles
  - Bore-by-bore lengths: 3,610 ft / 3,610 ft / 3,771 ft / 3,389 ft
- Sections: Quick Facts, name history, what changed in 2013, traffic patterns by time of day (school year vs summer, weekdays vs weekends), variables that matter (Giants games, graduation weekends, etc.), what happens when it closes, alternate routes (with strong "don't take surface streets" guidance), local quirks (faster lane choices, cell signal drops, radio cutouts, weather/microclimate at the portals), airport routing guidance, recent improvements, live conditions, cultural note
- Voice-matched to the practical-guide tone of the existing `getting-around/_index.md`, with a few local-knowledge sections that nod to the blog voice
- SEO-friendly meta description (188 chars)
- `lastVerified: "2026-05-29"` per the freshness-pattern rollout the 7am process started
